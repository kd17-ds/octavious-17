By 2050, drones are expected to play a significantly larger role in both agriculture and disaster management. In agriculture, the global adoption of drone technology for tasks such as crop monitoring, precision spraying, and yield estimation is anticipated to increase substantially. Currently, agricultural drones are employed in about 10-15% of farms worldwide. However, by 2050, this number could grow to 60-70%, driven by advances in drone technology, data analytics, and greater accessibility. This will enhance productivity, reduce labor costs, and promote more sustainable farming practices

In disaster management, drones are already used for search-and-rescue operations, delivering medical supplies, and mapping disaster zones. Currently, they are utilized in roughly 5-10% of disaster responses. By 2050, however, drones are expected to be integrated into up to 40-50% of disaster management efforts. This increase will be facilitated by advances in drone capabilities, better integration with other technologies (like AI and cloud computing), and improved regulation, making them indispensable tools for real-time disaster monitoring and response NIFA
